Experiment 2 - Vector Art, Animation, and Interactivity

Imitate

Over winter break, I decided to expose myself to more creative coding, to prepare myself for the kinds of things I would see in this class. My friend recommended this Instagram account, and I completely fell in love with their style. My main inspiration was this heart, because I was so fascinated by how it looked, and also because I love heart motifs.

Integrate

My original inspiration was only animation, but I also wanted to integrate some interactive elements into my own project. I really wanted to utilize the mouse position, like I had seen in other projects shown in class. I started by connecting lines from each of the dots to the mouse.

Innovate

The lines that I connected from the dots to the mouse reminded me of “tugging on heartstrings,” so I wanted to play around more with that idea. I also wanted to do more than just one thing with the mouse position. I got the idea to change the color of the heart and the strings based on the direction it's pulled, to symbolize how different things can make a heart feel differently. .

Reflection

I worked on this alone, and I am honestly pleasantly surprised with the result. Even though I consulted ChatGPT, I am very proud of my work since it's an idea that came from my own mind, and most of my code was my own. I did have some minor setbacks in not being able to get the rotation working, and figuring out how to even draw a heart, but after giving myself some time away from the project, I was able to complete the project, and I even received feedback from my friends on how they enjoy the project and its message.
