Experiment 3 - Generative Methods

Imitate

For this experiment, I was inspired by YOASOBI, even though they are musical artists. I decided to base my Generative Methods project on one of their music videos, since I have always loved the art and animation in them. After watching several, I finally decided on Halzion, due to the music video's aesthetic.

Integrate

I used both the random() and noise() functions in this experiment. I used noise for the size of the windows, so that each window size wouldn't differ too greatly from the last. I used random for many things - the colors picked for each gradient, the position of the windows, and of course, the number of sides for each shape, and the position of each line within the shape.

Innovate

My innovation was the way that I drew the random shapes that appear in each window. For each shape, I chose a random amount of sides that shape would have, and drew that many lines from each shape by choosing a random point for each vertex of the lines. I have never seen anything like this done before, and I think they made really cool scribbly shapes.

Reflection

I worked on this alone, and I am really pleased with the result. I love how it looks, and I really enjoyed the process of working on it, and I feel like I have also learned a lot about p5.js through the making of this project. It was a bit hard for me to get started and think of an idea at first, but once I did, I was able to work almost nonstop until I finished and was satisfied with the result.
